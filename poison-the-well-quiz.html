Bio / Context

Poison the Well emerged from South Florida’s late-90s hardcore scene and helped define melodic metalcore. Their debut LP, The Opposite of December… A Season of Separation, arrived on December 14, 1999 via Trustkill Records. It blended dual melodic guitars with crushing breakdowns, raw dynamics, and metaphor‑heavy lyrics about heartbreak and distance.

Across 28 fierce minutes, the record moves through fast-to-mid tempos, abrupt stops/starts, and sparing clean vocals that heighten the dominant screams. Tracks like “12/23/93,” “Nerdy,” “A Wish for Wings That Work,” and closer “My Mirror No Longer Reflects” became canon for a generation of heavy music fans.
